# Vēdic Language and Origin of Sanskrit

---

## Statement

**Vēdic language is the first complete language. From it, Sanskrit took shape, yet these two are distinct in form.**

---

## Essence of Yajur Vēda (7–14)

The Supreme Being (Paramātma) is the primordial one, existing before all creation. From Him emerged speech — the Vēda — which represents the earliest form of language.

This Vēda: Scared words or the language of the Vēdas
- Is the first of all languages  
- Bestows prosperity and knowledge  

O Lord, O Soma — For the sake of Your sacred knowledge of the Vēda, may we become

- Valiant like Varuṇa  
- Rich in learning and wealth like Agni, 
- Generous in giving like Mitra  

---

## Proposition

### Question 1: Which is the first language?  
**Answer:** The language of the Vēdas (Vēdic speech), as stated in Yajur Vēda (7–14).

---

### Question 2: What is indivisible?  
**Answer:** The Vēdic language, as indicated in Yajur Vēda (7–14).

---

### Question 3: Did Sanskrit originate from the Vēdic language?  
**Answer:** Yes.

Yajur Vēda (7–14)  it is said: “May we, like Mitra or Vāyu (RigVēda 3-59-9), be givers of offerings.” Just as Vāyu refines and purifies all substances, so too should we refine the Vēdic language and offer it as Sanskrit, the language of purity and refinement.

---

## Conclusion 

It is evident from Yajur Vēda (7–14) that:

- The Vēda is the first language of humans
- Sanskrit developed from the Vēdic language  
- Sanskrit language, containing those words, originated from it. 

Thus, Vēda is the **origin**, and Sanskrit is the **origination** from Vēda.
